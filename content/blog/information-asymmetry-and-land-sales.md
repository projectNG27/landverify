## What buyers rarely see

In many land deals, the seller controls which documents you see first. Survey copies, allocation letters, and payment receipts can be genuine yet **incomplete**: they may not show prior transfers, boundary disputes, or how the parcel relates to neighbouring claims.

## Why WhatsApp is not enough

Informal updates feel fast, but they skip structure. Without a checklist, it is easy to celebrate a “clean” file while missing obvious gaps—like a mismatch between the plan description and the road you actually visited.
